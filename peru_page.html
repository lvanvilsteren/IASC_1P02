My time in Peru

During the second half of my trip, a group of 16 other Canadians and I all got together in Montreal; which is where we started our journey. We took three different planes and eventually made it to Lima, Peru. We stayed in Lima for a couple nights, then took an eight-hour bus ride up the mountains to the city Huncyco. We then stayed there for a few nights as well, but then finally we made it to Orcotuna, which was going to be our new home for the next 2 months.

While we were in Oroctuna, we all lived in separate home’s, but the town was small enough that we could walk to everyone. The few were fantastic; the food was amazing, and the company was better then we could imagine. I live in a home with a mother, father, two daughters and two dogs. I was a different home compared to ones in Canada, but it had everything we could’ve ever needed.

While we were there, we got to do a lot of different things that kept us busy. One of the main things that we did was teach English, French and Art classes to the local summer school that was in process. It was fun meeting and trying to teach all the kids new things and fun seeing them pick up the little things. Since most of our Spanish wasn’t the greatest it was a learning curve for the both of us.

We also planted a garden at the school, as well as made a new playground for the kids that was made of solely recycled materials. But one of the main reasons we went there for was to build stoves and fridges for some of the locals who needed them. These items were made solely from adobe bricks, wood planks, and mud (composed of dirt, water, straw and pigs hair). We ended up only making 6 of each in total, due to the lack of time we had there. I loved it in Orcotuna, and I hope that one day when my Spanish gets better ill go back to visit.
